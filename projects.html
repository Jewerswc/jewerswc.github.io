Projects

A few of the things I’ve built. This is not a complete list, just a record of some projects that were useful, interesting, or taught me something.

legocharacters.com

A site that turns any image into a LEGO-style scene.

Useful mainly as a reminder that shipping simple consumer products teaches you a lot about interfaces, latency, and what people actually enjoy using.

Polyspark

An AI-powered long-form writing platform built around adaptive writer agents.

This taught me a lot about designing products around model behavior rather than pretending the model is the whole product.

News classification project

A news-classification project inspired by an idea from Paul Graham, and related to work by Andrej Karpathy, exploring hindsight analysis, forward prediction models, and spam filtering.

More than anything else, it pushed me to think harder about signal, noise, and what it means to build a system that is actually useful rather than merely technically interesting.

Booksorter

A Python classifier that predicts which unread books I’m most likely to actually read.

Small project, but a good one for thinking about ranking, taste, and how to formalize messy personal preferences.

Flood-zone mapping tool

A mapping tool designed to identify properties at risk of flooding.

A useful example of taking a real-world problem, finding the relevant data, and turning it into something clearer and more practical.
