What I do/what I've done:

What I do

I'm building Interesting Or Not, an online platform that helps early-stage writers find their most interested readers and tells them what those readers want.

I recently took part in Durham University's Venture Lab Startup Accelerator program [Jul-Sep 2024].

If you're interested in this, please reach out to me via: william-jewers@interestingornot.com.

What I've done

I've also been involved in UK politics previously working in the House of Commons as a researcher from the ages of 18 to 24. During that time, I contributed to this paper Conservatism in Crisis: Rise of the Bureaucratic Class.

I have an undergraduate degree (BA) in philosophy and politics (2023, Durham, UK). During that time, among other things, I wrote this paper on Edmund Burke's use of language: Burke's Three Doctrines And The French English Question Of Reflections.

I taught myself to code at age 20. I've built various things since. You can find my repositories via the Github widget below.
