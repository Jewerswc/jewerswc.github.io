What I do / What I’ve done

What I do

I’m building Parlyreply, an AI-native CX platform for the public sector.

Last year, I completed Durham University’s Venture Lab accelerator (Jul–Sep 2024), receiving £2,500 in grant funding to work on a startup idea.

What I’ve done

Before I got interested in startups and coding, I did some other things.

From 18–24 I intermittently worked in the UK House of Commons as a researcher. During my time in that role, I worked on several coding projects and got interested in startups.

I hold a BA in Philosophy & Politics (Durham ’23). During my degree I looked at Edmund Burke’s rhetoric in Burke’s Three Doctrines And The French English Question Of Reflections.

I’ve also taught myself to code and built the following projects (most recent first):

  • legocharacters.com — a site that turns any image into a LEGO-style scene.
  • Polyspark — an AI-powered long-form writing platform built around adaptive writer agents.
  • A news-classification project inspired by an idea from Paul Graham, and related to work by Andrej Karpathy, exploring hindsight analysis, forward prediction models, and spam filtering.
  • Booksorter — a Python classifier that predicts which unread books I’m likely to actually read.
  • A flood-zone mapping tool designed to identify properties at risk of flooding.

You can find the rest of my projects on GitHub above.
