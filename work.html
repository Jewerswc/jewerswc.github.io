Work

Current work, relevant background, and selected projects.

I’m a founder and self-taught builder. The work that suits me best tends to sit where software, institutions, and judgment-heavy workflows meet.

Current work

Building ParlyReply

I’m building ParlyReply, an AI-native CX platform for the public sector. More broadly, I’m interested in products that reduce institutional friction and make complex service workflows more usable.

Operating model

Product, design, and code

I like working across the full stack, especially when the product needs repeated design and implementation loops to feel right.

Recent signal

Durham Venture Lab

I completed Durham University’s Venture Lab accelerator from July to September 2024 and received £2,500 in grant funding to work on a startup idea.

Relevant background

Politics, research, then software

Before startups and product work took over, I spent several years in and around British politics. That period shaped how I think about institutions, communication, and the practical constraints real systems operate under.

Public institutions

House of Commons researcher

From 18 to 24 I intermittently worked in the UK House of Commons as a researcher. It was there that I started building small tools and got more serious about software and startups.

Education

Philosophy and politics

I hold a BA in Philosophy & Politics from Durham University (2023). My dissertation looked at Edmund Burke’s rhetoric in Burke’s Three Doctrines And The French English Question Of Reflections .

Selected projects

Projects that show how I think

These are not all the same kind of product, but they each show a useful part of the picture: shipping, experimentation, interface taste, and a bias toward building tools that are actually usable.

legocharacters.com

A consumer web product that turns any image into a LEGO-style scene; useful as proof that I like shipping internet products, not just talking about them.

Polyspark

An AI-powered long-form writing platform built around adaptive writer agents, and a serious exercise in product design around generative systems.

News classification project

A project inspired by Paul Graham and adjacent to work discussed by Andrej Karpathy, exploring hindsight analysis, forward prediction models, and spam filtering for news signals.

Booksorter

A Python classifier that predicts which unread books I’m actually likely to read, built as a small but concrete ranking problem.

Flood-zone mapping tool

A property-risk mapping project designed to identify homes with meaningful exposure to flooding.
