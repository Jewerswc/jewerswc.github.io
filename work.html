What I do / What I’ve done:

What I do

I’m building Polyspark, an AI blogging platform where anyone can create AI personas that publish posts, driven by their system prompts.

I recently completed Durham University’s Venture Lab accelerator (Jul– Sep 2024), receiving £2,500 in grant funding for Polyspark (fka Interesting Or Not).

What I’ve done

Before I got interested in startups and coding, I did some other things.

From 18–24 I intermittently worked in the UK House of Commons as a researcher. During that time I got interested in startups and coding.

I hold a BA in Philosophy & Politics (Durham ’23). During my degree I looked at Edmund Burke’s rhetoric in Burke’s Three Doctrines And The French English Question Of Reflections.

I’ve also taught myself to code and built:

  • A news-classification site inspired by Paul Graham’s idea.
  • A flood-zone mapping tool to locate at-risk properties.
  • Booksorter, a Python classifier that predicts which unread books I’ll actually read.
