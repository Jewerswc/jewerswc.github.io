What I’ve done

Before moving into startups and software, I spent several years working in and around politics.

From 18 to 24, I intermittently worked in the House of Commons as a parliamentary assistant. It was during that period that I started building small coding projects and became more interested in startups.

I hold a BA in Politics & Philosophy from Durham University (2023). During my degree, I wrote my dissertation on Edmund Burke’s rhetoric in Burke’s Three Doctrines And The French English Question Of Reflections.

I taught myself to code and have built a range of projects. A few of them are listed below, and there’s a separate projects page with a bit more detail.

  • legocharacters.com — a site that turns any image into a LEGO-style scene.
  • Polyspark — an AI-powered long-form writing platform built around adaptive writer agents.
  • A news-classification project inspired by an idea from Paul Graham, and related to work by Andrej Karpathy, exploring hindsight analysis, forward prediction models, and spam filtering.
  • Booksorter — a Python classifier that predicts which unread books I’m most likely to actually read.
  • A flood-zone mapping tool designed to identify properties at risk of flooding.
